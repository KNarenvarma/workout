# Weak-point programming research — verified findings (2026-07-02)

Deep-research pass: 5 search angles → 23 source-extraction agents → 25 falsifiable
claims → 3-vote adversarial verification each (2/3 refutations kill). 22 claims
survived, 3 killed. Findings below are the survivors, grouped by decision.

## Calves (the weak point)

- **Standing beats seated for visible calf mass.** 12-week within-subject trial:
  standing (knee-extended) raises grew lateral gastrocnemius +12.4% vs +1.7%
  seated, medial +9.2% vs +0.6%; seated produced *no significant* gastrocnemius
  hypertrophy (it loads the deeper soleus).
  [PMC10753835](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10753835/)
- **Train the stretch.** Lengthened-partial ROM (bottom, stretched portion) more
  than doubled medial gastroc growth vs full ROM over 8 weeks (+15.2% vs +6.7%;
  shortened partials +3.4%).
  [JSCR 2023](https://journals.lww.com/nsca-jscr/fulltext/2023/09000/greater_gastrocnemius_muscle_hypertrophy_after.3.aspx)
- **Volume drives growth; frequency per se doesn't.** With volume equated,
  extra frequency has negligible independent hypertrophy effect — spreading calf
  sets across the week is for set quality, not a frequency magic.
  [SportRxiv 460](https://sportrxiv.org/index.php/server/preprint/view/460)
- **Dose-response:** minimum effective ≈4 weekly sets; ~0.24%/set marginal gain
  at moderate volumes; 12–20 weekly sets proposed optimal for trained lifters;
  no clear plateau but >25 sets is extrapolation.
  [SportRxiv 460](https://sportrxiv.org/index.php/server/preprint/view/460),
  [PMC8884877](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8884877/)

**Program applied:** LB1 Standing Calf Raises 4×8–12 heavy (2s bottom pause) ·
LB2 Standing Calf Raises 4×12–20 (+bottom-stretch half-reps to failure; seated
kept as soleus alternative) · UB2 finisher 2×15–20 → **10 direct weekly sets**,
progressing upward if recovery allows.

## Upper chest (photo: delts/arms ahead of chest)

- Incline press produces greater upper-chest (clavicular) thickness than flat.
  [PubMed 32922646](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32922646/)
- Clavicular EMG higher at 32–43° than 20°; above ~45° the stimulus shifts to
  anterior deltoid. With already-strong delts, **~30° is the sweet spot**.
  [EU OpenSci 9255](https://www.eu-opensci.org/index.php/sport/article/view/9255),
  [PMC7579505](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7579505/)
- Killed claims (failed verification): "30° is *the* maximum-EMG angle", "43° is
  recommended over 32°", "grip width matters more than incline".

**Program applied:** UB2 Low Incline DB Press cue changed to ~30°.

## Maintaining strong points cheaply

- Strength: ~1–4 weekly sets is the effective floor-to-plateau band; single-set
  training on ≥2 days/wk measurably maintains/increases strength.
  [SportRxiv 460](https://sportrxiv.org/index.php/server/preprint/view/460),
  [PMC11127831](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11127831/)
- Size/strength can be held up to 32 weeks on 1 session/wk, 1 set/exercise, **if
  intensity is preserved**.
  [Spiering et al. 2021](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/349637321_Maintaining_Physical_Performance_The_Minimal_Dose_of_Exercise_Needed_to_Preserve_Endurance_and_Strength_Over_Time)
- Hypertrophy dose-response is graded (<5 / 5–9 / 10+ weekly sets), diminishing
  returns hit strength sooner than size.
  [Schoenfeld 2017](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/02640414.2016.1210197),
  [PubMed 41343037](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41343037/)

**Option (not yet applied):** if sessions run long, delt/biceps volume can drop
1 set each with minimal cost — those muscles are ahead and cheap to maintain.

## Time efficiency

- For time-crunched lifters, weekly volume matters more than how it's split;
  ≥4 weekly sets/muscle is the working floor.
  [PMC8449772](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8449772/)
